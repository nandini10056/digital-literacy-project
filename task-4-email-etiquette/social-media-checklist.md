# 📱 Social Media Guidelines for College Students

---

## ✅ Smart Moves (What You Should Do)

| # | Action | Purpose |
|---|--------|---------|
| 1 | Pause Before Posting | Once something goes online, it can be saved or shared forever. Always consider how it might look to teachers or employers. |
| 2 | Adjust Your Privacy Controls | Regularly review who can view your posts. Keep sensitive content limited to trusted circles. |
| 3 | Create a Positive Online Image | Highlight your skills, projects, achievements, and learning journey to build credibility. |
| 4 | Cross-Check Information | Avoid spreading false news. Confirm sources before sharing anything informative or news-related. |
| 5 | Be Mindful of Your Digital Identity | Maintain consistency in how you present yourself across platforms, especially on professional sites. |

---

## ❌ Risky Behaviors (What to Avoid)

| # | Behavior | Consequence |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Posting in the Heat of the Moment | Emotional posts can harm your image and lead to regret later. Take time to cool off first. |
| 2 | Revealing Too Much Personal Data | Sharing sensitive details can lead to privacy breaches, scams, or safety issues. |
| 3 | Getting Into Online Fights | Arguments rarely resolve online and can leave a negative impression on others. |
| 4 | Sharing Others’ Content Without Consent | Posting photos or tagging people without permission can violate their privacy. |
| 5 | Using Social Media in Academic Settings Improperly | It distracts from studies and can be seen as disrespectful or unethical during exams. |

---

## 📌 Platform-Wise Tips

### 🔹 LinkedIn (Career-Focused)
- ✔ Post about internships, certifications, and academic work  
- ✖ Avoid casual or overly personal content  

### 🔹 Instagram / Facebook (Personal Use)
- ✔ Share within a limited audience and review tagged content  
- ✖ Avoid public posts that could harm your reputation  

### 🔹 Twitter/X (Highly Public)
- ✔ Share ideas thoughtfully and engage respectfully  
- ✖ Avoid impulsive opinions or offensive remarks  

### 🔹 Messaging Apps (Private ≠ Secure)
- ✔ Verify messages before forwarding  
- ✖ Avoid spreading rumors or sharing private chats  

---

## 🌟 Simple Rule to Remember  

**"If it could affect your future, think twice before you share."**
