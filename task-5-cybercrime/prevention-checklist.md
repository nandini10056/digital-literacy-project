# 🛡️ Online Safety Guide: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for College Students

---

## 🔐 Basic Cyber Safety Practices

### 1. Create Strong Passwords
- Use at least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols  
- Avoid using the same password across multiple platforms  
- Consider using password managers (e.g., Bitwarden, Dashlane)  
- Update passwords if you suspect any breach  

### 2. Turn On Two-Step Verification
- Enable 2FA for email, banking, and social media accounts  
- Use authenticator apps instead of SMS when possible  
- Keep backup recovery codes stored safely  
- Never disclose OTPs to anyone  

### 3. Stay Alert Against Phishing
- Don’t click on unknown or suspicious links  
- Check email sender addresses carefully  
- Avoid responding to urgent messages asking for personal data  
- Always visit official websites directly instead of using links  

### 4. Keep Devices Updated
- Regularly update your phone, laptop, and apps  
- Security updates fix bugs and vulnerabilities  
- Remove apps you don’t use anymore  
- Install software only from trusted sources  

### 5. Use Safe Internet Connections
- Avoid financial transactions on public Wi-Fi  
- Use a trusted VPN when accessing sensitive data  
- Turn off auto-connect to open networks  
- Disable Bluetooth when not needed  

### 6. Limit Personal Information Sharing
- Don’t share phone numbers, addresses, or live locations publicly  
- Post travel updates after returning  
- Review privacy settings frequently  
- Avoid accepting requests from unknown profiles  

### 7. Confirm Before Sharing Information
- Banks never ask for PIN, OTP, or passwords via calls/messages  
- Verify suspicious calls by contacting official numbers  
- Avoid giving remote access of your device  
- Be cautious of fake tech support scams  

### 8. Monitor Your Accounts Regularly
- Check bank and UPI transactions frequently  
- Enable transaction alerts  
- Review login activity on accounts  
- Report anything unusual immediately  

---

## 💳 Digital Payment & Banking Safety

### 9. UPI Safety Tips
- Don’t send money for “account verification”  
- Accept requests only from trusted contacts  
- Double-check receiver details before payment  
- Set daily spending limits for safety  
- Save transaction receipts/screenshots  

### 10. Identify Refund/Transfer Scams
- Verify unexpected incoming money through your app  
- Be cautious if someone asks for a refund urgently  
- Never send extra money beyond what you received  
- Ignore emotional pressure tactics  

### 11. Protect Card Details
- Never share CVV or PIN with anyone  
- Use virtual cards for online payments if available  
- Turn on alerts for all transactions  
- Always hide keypad while entering PIN  

### 12. Shop Online Safely
- Use secure websites (HTTPS with lock symbol)  
- Prefer trusted platforms and sellers  
- Avoid unrealistic discounts or offers  
- Check reviews before buying  
- Keep records of orders and payments  

---

## 🚨 What to Do If You’re Scammed

### Immediate Actions
- Contact your bank to block transactions or cards  
- Take screenshots of all evidence (messages, payments, numbers)  
- Do not delete any suspicious communication  

### Report the Incident
- Call **1930** (Cyber Crime Helpline)  
- File complaint at **cybercrime.gov.in**  
- Report to your bank as soon as possible  
- Visit the nearest police station if needed  

### Secure Your Accounts
- Change all passwords immediately  
- Enable 2FA if not already active  
- Inform friends/contacts if your account was hacked  

### Keep Records
- Save complaint IDs and reports  
- Maintain proof of transactions and conversations  
- Note dates, times, and details of the incident

## 🎓 Tips for Students

- Use separate emails for academics and personal use  
- Be careful of fake internship or scholarship offers  
- Verify job opportunities through official websites  
- Avoid paying money for jobs or internships  
- Stay cautious of “easy money” schemes online

**"Think before you click, verify before you trust."**

A little caution can prevent major losses. Stay informed and stay secure.
