# Cybercrime Case Study: QR Code Payment Scam

---

## Case Study: The “Scan to Receive Money” Fraud

### What is This Type of Fraud?
This scam targets users of digital payment platforms like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm. Fraudsters trick victims into scanning a QR code under the false promise of receiving money, while in reality, the action authorizes a payment from the victim’s account.

---

## How the Scam Works (Step-by-Step)

**Step 1 – The Approach:**  
The victim is contacted through a phone call, WhatsApp message, or an online marketplace (such as OLX or Instagram). The scammer often pretends to be a buyer, recruiter, or company representative.

**Step 2 – Fake Payment Promise:**  
The fraudster claims they want to send money to the victim—for example, to buy a product, pay an advance, or issue a refund.

**Step 3 – Sharing the QR Code:**  
Instead of transferring money, the scammer sends a QR code and instructs the victim to scan it in order to “receive” payment.

**Step 4 – Creating Urgency:**  
The victim is pressured with statements like “this request will expire soon” or “please complete it quickly.”

**Step 5 – The Fraudulent Transaction:**  
When the victim scans the QR code and enters their UPI PIN, the money gets debited from their account instead of being credited.

**Step 6 – Disappearance:**  
After the transaction, the scammer becomes unreachable and stops responding.

---

## Common Targets

- Students and first-time digital payment users  
- People selling items online (OLX, Facebook Marketplace, etc.)  
- Job seekers expecting payments or reimbursements  
- Small business owners handling frequent transactions  
- Individuals unaware of QR code payment mechanics  

---

## Impact of the Scam

### On Individuals:
- Financial loss (typically ₹1,000 to ₹20,000)  
- Confusion and stress after the incident  
- Risk of further fraud if sensitive details are shared  

### On Society:
- Reduced trust in digital payment platforms  
- Slower adoption of cashless systems  
- Increased workload for banks and cybercrime authorities  

---

## Real-Life Insight

Reports in India indicate a steady increase in QR-based payment frauds, especially with the rise of online marketplaces and digital transactions. Many cases occur due to a simple misconception that scanning a QR code is required to receive money.

---

## Key Lesson

**"You never need to scan a QR code to receive money—only to pay."**
